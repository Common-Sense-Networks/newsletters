December 2022
 

Last year's suggestions for fun ways to celebrate New Year’s Eve as a family were so popular that we are sharing them again!
  • Dress up in your favorite fancy clothes.
  • Make ‘2023’ glasses or headbands using pipe cleaners. 
  • Create a photo opp—with your own 2023 custom backdrop to commemorate the night. 
  • Write out new year’s resolutions—revisit them next year to see how you did! 
  • Have a dance party.
  • Watch a slideshow of photos and videos from the past year.
  • Toast to the new year with a sparkling family-friendly drink—add seltzer to any juice.
  • Need to get the kids to bed? Celebrate midnight in an earlier time zone!

Mouk

In Search of the Yeti: In Tibet, Mouk and Chavapa visit a monastery in the mountains. They hope to take part in a traditional New Year celebration, but will they also meet the legendary Yeti creature?

Based on the popular book series, Mouk and his friend Chavapa travel around the world discovering new cultures and places. Emphasizing local traditions, customs, and music, kids learn about embracing differences and being open to trying new things.

Benefits: Positive Messages • Social Studies

Kikoriki

New Year Fairytale: Rosa wants to have a big New Year's party, but Barry is hibernating. Who will wake him up?

Nine, cute (and round) critters live and work together. Each story illustrates how members of the community support each other. Stories tackle introverts and extroverts, optimism and pessimism, health, moral lessons, and safety.

Benefits: Imagination, Games & Play • Social Emotional Learning
